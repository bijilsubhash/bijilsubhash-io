What I Do

I am the founder of NimbleStax, where I work as a fractional data engineer helping early-stage startups and mid-tier organisations build out their data platforms from the ground up. My work spans the full spectrum of the modern data stack - from ingestion and transformation to orchestration, governance, and infrastructure as code.

I specialise in designing and deploying end-to-end data platforms using tools like dbt, Dagster, dlt, BigQuery, Snowflake, Databricks, Terraform, and Python. Whether it is migrating off legacy infrastructure, building config-driven ingestion frameworks, or rolling out cost-effective architectures on GCP, AWS and Azure, I focus on delivering solutions that are maintainable, extensible, and built to last.

My Approach

I consider myself to be a pragmatic engineer who is not afraid to fail and always willing to learn from my mistakes. I often have to make decisions that are both functional and cost-effective while keeping in mind that generating a positive ROI as early as possible is a win for everyone. I am a strong advocate for clean and efficient code, and I always aim to deliver high-quality solutions that exceed expectations.

Background

Over the past few years, I have worked on a diverse range of projects, from early-stage startups to large enterprise solutions, across various sectors including energy, retail, cybersecurity, finance, and marketing. These include building event-driven architectures processing hundreds of millions of rows daily, enterprise-scale migrations from Teradata and Salesforce to cloud-native platforms, and deploying GenAI pipelines for natural language processing.
